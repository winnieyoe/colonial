The naming of streets and roads is arbitrary, in fact, it often embeds hints of a place’s local history. Many of the street names in Hong Kong reflects its colonial past (1800s — 1997), with streets being named after British royal family and officials, Chinese and expatriate elites in Hong Kong, local and foreign enterprises, while others are named after industries, historical sites, and Chinese cities and provinces.
